CHOOSE YOUR DESTINATIONS

We offer to all our customers a custom designed itenerary, accordingly to your wishes and dreams. Here are some of the multiple place you can visit on board our motorhomes, ordered from North to South.

Galicia, Spain

Galicia has a lot to offer. Only 1 and a half hour drive from Porto you'll find a lot of places to explore, from the marvelous beaches in Cangas, to the wild islands Cies, including some beautiful cities like Vigo, Pontevedra, and A Coruña.

Vila Nova de Cerveira

A picturesque town, on the shore of Rio Lima, with a very nice historic center. You can choose to sleep on the Motorhome park, close to the center, or in a beautiful spot on top of the river.

Video

Viana do Castelo

Viana do Castelo is a very beautiful city, with a big shore. Perfect for a one day visit (which must include a visit to the Santa Lucia church) or to sleep on its shore and take advantage of it's multiple surf spots. We recommend to sleep in Cabedelo or on on Forte do Paço beach.

PORTO

Porto was selected as the best European destination for a break. Lose yourself on Porto streets, taste the port wine and see its famous bridges.

In Porto, you can either sleep near the river, in a fisherman village or on a campsite. We will show you all these spots.

We strongly recommend to take some bicycles, specially eBikes, to explore the old city and the famous Porto architectural buildings.

Nazaré

Well known for the world biggest waves and a surf spot, Nazaré is a very picturesque fishing town that's worth visiting.
In Nazaré be sure to visit "o Sitio", especially the top view over the city and the lighthouse.
To sleep you can choose wild camping on the north beach, a very good campsite or a (sometimes crowded) motorhome park on the center of the city.

Óbidos

A lovely castle town, with very beautiful streets. It's one of the most well-preserved examples of medieval architecture.

You will find a well preserved castle within the walls, and a maze of streets and white houses that are a delight to stroll amongst. Along with the Manueline porticoes, the colourful window boxes and the small squares, many fine examples of religious and civil architecture from the town’s golden days provide a host of reasons for making a visit.

It has a motorhome campsite, just at the town entrance, with all the necessary services.

ALENTEJO

Wake up with the sound of flowing water. Take the kayak and explore the wilderness. Stop by an old town. Take the bikes and explore the streets and its people. Admire the sunset at the beach. Take the surfboard and challenge yourself.
